1. Introduction

NeoLauncher is designed to provide launcher, search, widget, and personalization features while keeping data collection as limited as possible. Most app data remains on your device.

2. Information We Collect

The app may access or process the following categories of information:

  • Crash diagnostics such as crash reports and technical error data.
  • Contacts, if you grant permission, to support contact search features.
  • Calendar information, if you grant permission, to display event-related features.
  • Location data, if you grant permission, to provide weather functionality.
  • Notification-related data, if you enable the feature, to show unread badge counts.
  • Battery state, for battery ring and related overlay features.
  • User-entered search queries when using search or web suggestion features.
  • App settings and layout preferences stored locally on your device.
Most information used by NeoLauncher stays on your device and is processed locally.

3. How We Use Information

NeoLauncher uses information only to provide and improve app features, including:

  • Displaying apps, contacts, and calendar events
  • Providing weather information
  • Supporting search and web suggestions
  • Maintaining launcher settings and layout
  • Improving app stability and fixing crashes

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data.

Some data may be shared with external services only when necessary to provide specific features:

  • Firebase Crashlytics for crash reporting and diagnostics
  • Open-Meteo for weather data requests
  • Nominatim / OpenStreetMap for location-related lookup
  • Search providers such as Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Yahoo, or Brave when you use web search features

5. Data Storage

NeoLauncher stores most data locally on your device, such as settings, workspace layout, and personalization choices.

Some technical information, such as crash reports, may be transmitted securely to Firebase Crashlytics.

6. Permissions

NeoLauncher may request permissions to enable optional features:

  • Contacts for contact search
  • Calendar for event display
  • Location for weather
  • Notifications access for unread badge counts
  • Accessibility access for overlay-related features such as battery ring and edge lighting

If you deny any permission, the related feature may be limited or unavailable, but the app can still function.

7. Your Choices

  • You can deny or revoke permissions at any time in Android system settings.
  • You can reset app data using in-app reset options, where available.
  • You can uninstall the app to remove locally stored data from your device.

8. Data Security

We take reasonable steps to protect information by limiting collection, keeping most data local, and using established third-party services only where needed.

9. Children’s Privacy

NeoLauncher is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

10. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Any updates will be reflected by revising the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.
